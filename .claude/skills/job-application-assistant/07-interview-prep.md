# Interview Preparation Guide

## STAR Format

Structure answers as: **Situation** (context), **Task** (your responsibility), **Action** (what you did), **Result** (outcome).

Keep answers to 1-2 minutes. Be specific. End with what you learned or would do differently.

## Ready-Made STAR Examples

### 1. Deal transaction verification and controls review, Dilawri Group of Companies
**S:** In the Deal Analyst role, vehicle sale transactions needed to be posted accurately in CDK and One-Eighty with complete supporting documentation.
**T:** Pushpak was responsible for verifying deal details, documentation, approvals, and authorizations before transactions entered accounting records.
**A:** He reviewed supporting documents, checked required approvals, compared deal details to vehicle costs and sale proceeds, and followed up with stakeholders when documentation or pricing details were missing or inconsistent.
**R:** This process helped keep 100+ monthly transactions accurate, complete, and audit-ready while preventing incomplete or unauthorized deals from entering the accounting records.
**Use for:** "Tell me about your attention to detail", "Describe your controls experience", "How do you handle incomplete documentation?", "Why IT audit?"

### 2. Reconciliation and variance analysis, Dilawri Group of Companies
**S:** Deal records needed to align with supporting documentation and sale proceeds for accurate month-end reporting.
**T:** Pushpak needed to identify discrepancies and ensure financial records were complete and reliable.
**A:** He compared deal details, vehicle costs, and proceeds against supporting documents, investigated variances, and communicated with cross-functional stakeholders to resolve exceptions.
**R:** The work supported accurate month-end reporting and strengthened the completeness of financial records.
**Use for:** "Tell me about a time you found an error", "How do you investigate discrepancies?", "Describe your reconciliation experience."

### 3. IBM Maximo data validation, Transdev Canada
**S:** Enterprise asset records in IBM Maximo needed to remain accurate for buildings, equipment, and vehicles.
**T:** Pushpak was responsible for validating records and improving data quality and audit trail integrity.
**A:** He reconciled IBM Maximo records against Excel files using VLOOKUP and PivotTables, identified variances, documented corrections, and coordinated remediation with stakeholders.
**R:** His work improved asset record accuracy, strengthened reporting reliability, and preserved a clearer audit trail.
**Use for:** "Describe your data quality experience", "Tell me about a time you improved reporting reliability", "How have you worked with enterprise systems?"

### 4. Standardizing data capture through Excel reports and validation rules, Transdev Canada
**S:** Operational information needed more consistent field-level capture.
**T:** Pushpak needed to help standardize data capture and reporting.
**A:** He built Excel reports and data validation rules to make data entry more consistent and easier to review.
**R:** The approach increased consistency in operational information and supported cleaner downstream reporting.
**Use for:** "Tell me about a process improvement", "How do you improve data quality?", "What tools have you used to support reporting?"

## Common Tough Questions

### "Why are you moving from dealership accounting into IT audit?"
> My accounting work has shown me how much reliable reporting depends on systems, documentation, approvals, and data quality. At Dilawri, I verify transactions in CDK and One-Eighty, review approvals and supporting documents, and resolve discrepancies before records are finalized. At Transdev, I validated IBM Maximo records and preserved audit trails. IT audit is the natural next step because it combines my B.Com, computer science degree, CPA path, and interest in controls and system reliability.

### "You do not have direct IT audit experience. Why should we consider you?"
> I would be early in formal IT audit methodology, but I already work with the building blocks that audit teams test: system records, source documentation, approvals, reconciliations, data integrity, and exception follow-up. I can bring disciplined review habits and learn the firm's audit framework, workpapers, and control testing methods quickly.

### "What is your biggest technical gap?"
> My resume does not yet show hands-on ownership of formal ITGC or SOX testing. I am building toward that area, and I can connect it to work I have already done: reviewing approvals and authorizations, preserving audit trails, reconciling system records, and documenting corrections. I would focus early onboarding on the organization's control framework, evidence standards, and testing methodology.

### "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
> I want to be a strong IT audit, controls, or technology risk professional who can evaluate systems from both the accounting and technical sides. My goal is to keep progressing through the CPA path while building practical experience in controls, data integrity, and technology-enabled assurance.

### "Why this company specifically?"
> Customize per company. Reference the firm's audit, risk, technology assurance, data governance, or controls practice. Do not use a generic answer.

## Questions You Should Ask Interviewers

### About the Role
- "What types of controls or systems would this role work with most often?"
- "What would success look like in the first 6 months for someone transitioning into IT audit?"
- "How much of the role is documentation review, testing, stakeholder interviews, and remediation follow-up?"

### About Training and Team Structure
- "How do you train new team members on your audit methodology and evidence standards?"
- "Would this role support CPA development or exposure to assurance work?"
- "How are responsibilities divided between junior analysts, seniors, and managers?"

### About Tools and Methods
- "Which GRC, audit documentation, ERP, or data analysis tools does the team use?"
- "How does the team approach data quality issues discovered during testing?"
- "What types of systems are most common in your client base or internal environment?"

## Phone/Video Interview Tips
- Have STAR examples written out.
- Keep the transition narrative concise: accounting plus computer science plus systems/data controls.
- Acknowledge gaps honestly, then bridge to transferable experience.
- Avoid claiming direct IT audit work unless it is clearly framed as a target or learning area.
- End with: "Is there anything else about my background that I can clarify?"

## After the Application
- If the employer provided a timeline, wait until that timeline passes before following up.
- If no timeline was given and two weeks have passed, send a brief status inquiry.
- After interviews, send a concise thank-you note that references one role-specific detail from the conversation.
